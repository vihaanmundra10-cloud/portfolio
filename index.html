About

Research Interests

My research focuses on neurodegenerative disease mechanisms, computational biology, and simulation-based biomedical analysis. I am particularly interested in Parkinson’s disease, the gut-brain axis, alpha-synuclein pathology, and the intersection between neuroscience and data science.

I independently conduct literature reviews, statistical analysis, computational simulations, and biological modeling projects using public clinical datasets and scientific research. Alongside research, I advocate for neurological disease awareness through science communication and educational writing.

Research

Projects

Parkinson’s Disease, Gut Microbiome, and Gastrointestinal Pathways

Analyzed clinical metadata from 724 patients to investigate correlations between Parkinson’s disease and gastrointestinal dysfunction. Identified major metabolic pathway alterations, including phytol degradation and sulfur oxidation depletion, while proposing hypothetical dietary interventions related to oxidative stress and gut inflammation.

Clinical Dataset Analysis

Curcumin-Infused Nicotine Replacement Therapy Model

Developed a conceptual cardiovascular risk simulation exploring whether curcumin-infused nicotine replacement therapy could reduce coronary artery disease risk during smoking cessation. Built mathematical models comparing smokers, non-smokers, traditional NRT, and curcumin-assisted NRT.

View Simulation

Heart Disease Risk Simulator

Built an interactive cardiovascular disease simulator using public datasets to model heart disease risk factors and visualize disease progression trends through computational analysis and predictive modeling.

Open Simulator

Monte Carlo Cardiovascular Simulation

Expanded previous cardiovascular research by implementing probabilistic Monte Carlo simulations to model variability in cardiovascular outcomes and predict disease-response distributions under different treatment assumptions.

View Project

Alpha-Synuclein Mutation Modeling (SNCA Gene)

Conducted computational mutation analysis on the SNCA gene to explore how structural protein alterations in alpha-synuclein may contribute to neurodegenerative disease progression and Parkinson’s pathology.
